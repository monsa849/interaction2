Roots are long,

They dig in deep.

My body like bark, My hips so rough,
I’m dancing to the wind,
In lifetime.

To each her own.
Together, alone.
Breathing, beating, changing growth.
The giving tree.
Is that me?
Shel Silverstein—
hear him pump blood in me!
My spine gets cold, my arms grow old. I was planted shallow and now reach out
to spurt.


Oceanic fish in the sea,

Kangaroos go orange
against Arizona's midnight sky.
Twinkle twinkle little lights
Oh-
How I love to dance
with fireflies.

on a winter’s day
we watch your breath
fade to gray
I see children dance and play.

My crown is electric. Blue.
Blue.
Gold, brown, orange, and blue.
Hear me make love to you.
Touch my soul.
the ring around my poem.
It is so blue. And you
you are too.

Purple and pink melt into
the sunset's ink
a palette on painter's trays.
In case I don't get to see you again
I will say
Adieu.
